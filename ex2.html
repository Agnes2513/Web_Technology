Steps to Take Before a Disaster

Create an Emergency Plan

  • Communication Plan: Ensure all family members know how to contact each other in an emergency. Designate an out-of-town contact person.
  • Meeting Points: Choose safe locations where family members can meet if separated, both inside and outside your neighborhood.

Build an Emergency Kit

  • Essentials: Include water, non-perishable food, a flashlight, batteries, a first aid kit, medications, important documents, and cash.
  • Personal Needs: Pack items specific to your family’s needs, such as baby supplies, pet food, and extra clothes.

Prepare Your Home

  • Safety Measures: Secure heavy furniture, appliances, and water heaters to prevent them from tipping over.
  • Emergency Shut-Offs: Learn how to turn off your home’s gas, water, and electricity.
  • Evacuation Routes: Identify and practice evacuation routes from your home, workplace, and community.

Stay Informed

  • Weather Alerts: Sign up for local weather alerts and warnings.
  • Disaster Types: Know the types of disasters common in your area (e.g., floods, earthquakes, cyclones) and understand the risks associated with each.

Steps to Take After a Disaster

Ensure Safety

  • Check for Injuries: Look for injuries to yourself and others. Provide first aid if necessary and seek medical help for serious injuries.
  • Check for Hazards: Look out for hazards like downed power lines, gas leaks, or structural damage. Avoid entering damaged buildings.

Seek Assistance

  • Emergency Services: Contact your insurance company as soon as possible to report damage. Provide them with detailed information and photos.
  • Shelters and Aid: Apply for government disaster assistance programs if available in your area.

Document and Report

  • Insurance Claims: Contact your insurance company as soon as possible to report damage. Provide them with detailed information and photos.
  • Government Assistance: Apply for government disaster assistance programs if available in your area.
